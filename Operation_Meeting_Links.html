Root cause analysis - Incident description

A contractor was cutting the wires of a cable on an electric cabinet in plant A to decommission the cabinet. While doing this, he cut each wire separately and not all together. He found out that there was still 380V on the cable. The cable was not shown on the electrical drawing and was installed a long time ago. Cutting the power to the cabinet was not 100% effective as the cable in question was a separate cable not going through the main electrical switch which cuts the power to all the cables in the cabinet. The contractor was not hurt, but only because he cut one wire at a time. If he would have cut all the wires of the cable at once, then he would have been electrocuted. A LOTO procedure was used, but because the cable in question did not go through the main switch, the LOTO was ineffective.
